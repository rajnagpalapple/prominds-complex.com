Ginkgo Biloba is one of the oldest living tree species and is used in supplements for its purported ability to improve blood flow to the brain and act as an antioxidant, potentially aiding in focus and memory.

Huperzine

Huperzine is extracted from the Chinese club moss and is utilized for its possible neuroprotective qualities and its role in improving levels of neurotransmitters associated with memory and learning.

Tyrosine

Tyrosine is an amino acid that serves as a precursor to several important neurotransmitters like dopamine and norepinephrine, which are involved in cognitive processes such as memory and alertness.

Phosphatidylserine

Phosphatidylserine is a phospholipid that plays a crucial role in maintaining cellular function in the brain, and supplementation is thought to support cognitive functions like memory and concentration.

St. John’s Wort

St. John’s Wort is commonly known for its use in natural remedies for depression, but it is also believed to have neuroprotective properties that may support cognitive health and mental well-being.
7

N-Acetyl-L-Carnitine

N-Acetyl-L-Carnitine is a form of L-carnitine, which is related to the B vitamins and is involved in mitochondrial energy metabolism, potentially aiding in reducing cognitive decline and improving mental clarity.
8

Vinpocetine

Vinpocetine is derived from the periwinkle plant and is included for its potential to enhance cerebral blood flow, thereby possibly improving memory and cognitive function.
9

L-Glutamine

L-Glutamine is an amino acid that serves as a precursor to the neurotransmitter glutamate, which is involved in cognitive functions such as learning and memory.
10

Acetyl-L-Carnitine

Acetyl-L-Carnitine, similar to N-Acetyl-L-Carnitine, is involved in energy production and is also believed to have antioxidant properties, potentially aiding in the prevention of age-related cognitive decline.
